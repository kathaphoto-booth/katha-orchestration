Read me first

Your site is the brand law.

You own the storefront. Nothing here overrides what you build.

The pieces we made are a light coexistence layer, not a cage. Your Squarespace design choices stand — your fonts, your palette, your sections, your photographs. The brand layer catches a couple of narrow things the old "OAX" identity left behind (a pure-black overlay, some legacy text) and gives you ready-made signature pieces you can drop in where you want a Katha moment. Beyond that, build it the way you see it.

One ask: the voice and the CTA labels are the two things worth keeping consistent across both surfaces, because that's what makes the hand-off from your site to the booking portal feel like one brand. The quick reference at the bottom is the whole rulebook on a page.

The Architecture · in plain terms

Two surfaces, one brand.

There are two websites working together. You own the first. The second does the booking machinery.

The storefront

kathabooth.com

Your Squarespace site. The face of the brand — story, installations, galleries, the first impression. You design and run it. The brand layer (two paste-in files) keeps it quietly on-palette.

The booking portal

book.kathabooth.com

The working engine. When a guest taps "Begin Your Inquiry," they land here — a short form, template choices, and (soon) a live calendar. Inquiries come to you and Jed by email.

Every booking button on your site points to the portal. A client never feels the seam — same fonts, same voice, same colours. That's the whole point of keeping the two consistent.

The Workflow

How a client gets to a booked date.

As close to automatic as it can be — while you still press send.

01

They find you

A guest lands on kathabooth.com, reads the story, sees the installations, and taps Begin Your Inquiry.

02

They inquire

They arrive at the portal and leave the essentials — name, email, event date. Nothing heavy.

03

You hear about it

You and Jed get the inquiry by email the moment it's sent. No dashboard to babysit — it comes to your inbox.

04

You reply & propose

You review, then send the proposal — the smart file: invoice, payment, contract, all in one (HoneyBook). This is the one place a human decides; you press send.

05

They sign & pay

The client signs and pays inside the smart file. A thank-you note goes out. The date is held.

06

You build it

Installation day. The booth, the prints, the hand-operated craft — the part only you two do.

Coming next: a branded calendar inside the portal so clients can see open dates, and the smart-file flow tightened to a hybrid (invoice → payment → contract). The principle stays: simple, and you keep the final send.

What Changed · and why

The refinements, in the open.

We tightened the copy and the structure of the storefront draft. Nothing here is precious — push back on anything. Here's every meaningful change, before → after.

1 · Voice — jargon out, craft in

The biggest pass. We removed words that read as marketing rather than craft — and the repeated word "architecture," which was doing a lot of heavy lifting.

Before"Editorial booth architecture." · "your luxury photo booth experience." · "curated and delivered." · "output is timeless." · "align with your aesthetic."
After"Editorial portraiture, built by hand." · "operated by hand throughout your event." · "finished and handed to you." · "ages the way good photography should." · "with your vision."

2 · Headlines — plainer, stronger

Before"EDITORIAL BOOTH ARCHTECURE" (typo) · "Booth Architectures" · "CURATED ARCHITECTURE"
After"Editorial portraiture, built by hand." · "Choose Your Installation" · "Our Installations"

3 · One set of buttons, everywhere

Every call-to-action now draws from a single set — so your site and the portal speak the same way.

Begin Your Inquiry Request a Proposal Reserve Your Date Send Inquiry
Commission KTHA Request Bespoke Proposal Submit

4 · Structure

BeforeFounders as name + role lines with face-photo placeholders · tiers as flat stacked cards · a "Venues" section · a fake "what changed" toggle
AfterFounders as two text-led manifesto cards (no face photos until you have real ones) · tiers as a tap-to-expand accordion · "Venues" removed (no real sponsorships to claim) · this workbook replaces the toggle
Flag for you: two testimonials were lightly softened to drop "curation / experience." Please confirm with those clients before they go live — we don't want to put words in their mouths.

Quick Reference · the whole rulebook

If you remember nothing else.

Palette

Obsidian Weave#111112base / text
Piña Ecru#EAE2D5light ground
Loko Rust#8C382Asacred CTA only · ≤1 per screen
Capiz Sage#B5B8A3soft accent
Champagne#C4B59Don dark only
Walnut#574C3Fbody on light
Never pure black (#000) or pure white (#fff). Rust is the one sacred button colour — one per screen, nothing else.

Type

Now: Playfair Display for headlines, Hanken Grotesk for body — free near-matches that read like the real thing. Soon: the licensed pair you flagged — IvyMode (headlines, via Adobe Fonts) + Proxima Nova (body, via Canva/Adobe). The site is already wired so the swap is one line — the moment the Adobe Fonts kit is added, it switches with zero rework.

Voice

Lean in

  • Plain craft language — DSLR, archival cotton, hand-operated, on-site print
  • Peer-to-peer, calm, specific
  • "Curated" / "Handcrafted" — fine, but ≤3 times per page

Leave out

  • luxury, premium, experience, keepsake, timeless
  • curating / curation / elevate, journey, stunning, magical, authentic
  • "architecture" as filler — vary with installation, booth, build, form

Marks

Two marks only — the wordmark (katha) and the logo mark (the leaf/feather "K"). There is no maker's-mark stamp / brass ring. Your storefront simply uses the text header KATHA BOOTH — that's correct and canon.
