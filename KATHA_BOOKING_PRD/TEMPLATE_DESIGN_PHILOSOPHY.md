# LOOM & VOID
### A design philosophy for the Katha curated print-template system · 2026-07-04

---

**The movement.** Loom & Void is the discipline of the woven dark: compositions built the way a Filipina master weaver builds cloth — one warp line at a time, with patience that shows. Every artifact reads as an object that took countless hours: openwork drawn thread by thread, star fields plotted point by point, hairlines ruled by a steady hand at the absolute top of its craft. Nothing is decorated; everything is *constructed*. The viewer should feel the labor the way one feels the weight of good paper.

**Space and form.** The photograph apertures are sacred voids — they are never filled, never simulated, never explained. All composition serves them. Decoration lives at the margins and seams: the selvage of the cloth, not its field. Geometry is orthogonal and calm — rectangles in tension, a single bisecting thread, a pedestal of quiet text space. Asymmetry is permitted only in the chrome (fukinsei at the edges); the photo grid itself keeps perfect symmetry, the way a loom keeps its warp true.

**Color and material.** Each piece owns its ground the way paper owns its fiber: unbleached ecru, leaf-black void, roasted terracotta, deckled ivory, gilded bone. Color arrives as *material*, not tint — a red thread through dark cloth, gold leaf pressed into a rule, ink sinking into cotton. Accents are scarce and load-bearing; one thread of Achuete does the work that lesser hands would ask of ten. High contrast is earned through darkness and light, never through saturation.

**Rhythm and repetition.** Meaning accumulates through patient, systematic repetition — the calado's pierced openwork repeating like a prayer, star coordinates dense as a night sky over Carson, warp lines counted not estimated. Generative pattern is welcome when it behaves like handwork: deterministic, meticulous, slightly irregular in the way of human thread tension — never random noise, never machine jitter. A pattern must reward sustained viewing: from arm's length, texture; from close, individual decisions.

**Hierarchy and voice.** Typography is the engraver's voice: thin, spaced, certain. Names are set large enough to be the heirloom; everything else — dates, venues, registry marks — recedes to whisper-scale tracked capitals. Text never competes with the voids. A plate number, a hairline, a tiny footnote: these are the signatures of a registry that documents love the way a natural-science archive documents specimens — with reverence, precision, and restraint.

**The standard.** Every composition must look labored over — master-level execution, painstaking attention, zero placeholder gestures. If an element could have been made by default settings, it does not belong. The final artifact should stand in a museum drawer beside 19th-century embroidery sample cards and not be embarrassed. That is the bar.

*Filed for the template-design executor. The philosophy guides; the craft decides.*
