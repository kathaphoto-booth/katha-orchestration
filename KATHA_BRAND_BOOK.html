01 — THE STORY

The word is a verb.

Katha (Tagalog) — to compose, to invent, to weave a poem or story. Not a noun. The act of pressing raw earth — fiber, clay, wood — through the patience of the hand until it becomes an heirloom.

Three ancestral parents

Barong Nipis — sheer piña, calado openwork, sombrado shadow appliqué. Piña cannot be spun; every filament is hand-knotted. Transparency is refinement.

Inabel / Binakul — Ilocos optical weave; apotropaic geometry of wind and whirlpool, woven to confuse malevolent spirits.

T'nalak — T'boli dream-woven abacá; black/red/white from soil, blood, and raw fiber. When sold, a brass ring is offered — permission to leave the loom.

The throughline

Perseverance composed into thread. Every photo is not a captured moment — it is a stroke woven into a passed-down keepsake.

The booth is the loom. The print is a Katha. The KTHA mark is the brass ring — the seal that says this is ready to leave our hands.

05 — IMAGERY DIRECTION

Furniture-as-philosophy. Fabric-as-protagonist.

EVERY IMAGE BEHIND A DECKLED EDGE · NEVER SHARP RECTANGLES

Shoot

Hands at work — tying ribbon, threading paper, the shutter. The booth as object on raw plaster, golden side-light. Keepsakes in domestic context. Real guests, candid.

Don't shoot

Sparklers, neon, balloon arches, "fun" props. Retouched skin. Flash-bounce booth interiors. Stock photography, ever.

Treatment

Warm umber midtones, lifted shadows, no crushed blacks. Iron Bark shadows + Champagne highlights. 4:5 / 3:4 vertical; 16:9 hero only.
